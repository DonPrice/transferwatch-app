Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

✅ The short version

TransferWatch keeps your data under your control. Your calendar is read on-device, flight lookups send only flight numbers, and booking and message data syncs only with your own self-hosted server. Nothing is sold, shared, or sent to analytics or advertising companies.

1. Overview

TransferWatch ("the app") is developed by Donovan Price for use by airport transfer operators. This privacy policy explains what data the app accesses, where it goes, and your rights as a user.

The app does not create user accounts and does not use analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind. It communicates with exactly two kinds of endpoints: aviation data services that receive flight numbers only (Sections 3–4), and — only if you configure them — your own self-hosted companion services that you operate for your business (Section 5). No data is ever sent to any other party.

2. Calendar Access

TransferWatch requests full access to your device's calendar in order to:

  • Read event titles, notes, and locations to detect flight numbers
  • Display booking details (client name, address, phone) within the app
  • Monitor changes to calendar events in real time

Calendar content is read locally and used to display information within the app. It is not transmitted off your device, with one exception under your control: if you use the optional messaging features (Section 5), booking details you choose to include in a message — for example a pickup confirmation SMS — are delivered through your own self-hosted server.

3. Flight Data — AeroDataBox

To look up live flight status, TransferWatch sends flight numbers (e.g. QF404) to AeroDataBox, a third-party aviation data service accessed via the API.market gateway. The only information sent is the flight identifier and a date window.

No personal information is included in these requests. Client names, phone numbers, addresses, and calendar notes are never sent to AeroDataBox or API.market.

The AeroDataBox API key you enter in Settings is stored only in your device's local storage and is sent only with these flight lookups, to the API.market gateway endpoint that serves AeroDataBox.

See AeroDataBox's privacy policy at aerodatabox.com/privacy and API.market's at api.market/privacy_policy.

4. Optional Codeshare Lookup — AirLabs

If you enable the optional codeshare fallback in Settings (off by default), the app resolves marketing codeshare flight numbers by sending the flight number only, together with your AirLabs API key, to AirLabs (airlabs.co). Results are cached on your device so each codeshare number is looked up at most once. No personal information is included in these requests.

AirLabs' privacy policy is available at airlabs.co.

5. Self-Hosted Companion Services

TransferWatch can optionally connect to companion services that you host and operate yourself for your transfer business. Both are off by default and only activate when you enter your own server address and access token in Settings. They are not operated by any outside company, and no data sent to them leaves your control:

  • Bookings & messaging server (TransferBookings) — syncs customer SMS conversations and booking requests between the app and your server. This includes customer names, mobile numbers, message content, and booking details. Messages you send from the app are delivered by your server.
  • Flight push relay (flightboard) — registers your device's Apple push notification tokens with your server so it can send you flight-status alerts and Live Activity updates through the Apple Push Notification service, even when the app is closed.

Data stored on these servers is governed by you as their operator. If you never configure these services, the app makes no connection to them.

6. Notifications

TransferWatch delivers alerts about flight status changes, reminders, and delays. Notifications are generated on your device; if you have configured the optional push relay (Section 5), your own server also sends notifications remotely through the Apple Push Notification service. No outside company composes, receives, or processes notification content.

7. Data Stored on Your Device

The app stores the following data locally on your device using Apple's standard app storage:

  • Your selected calendar identifiers
  • Your AeroDataBox API key, and your AirLabs API key if you use the codeshare fallback
  • The addresses and access tokens of your self-hosted servers, if configured
  • Cached flight status data and codeshare lookups (to persist between app launches)
  • Your message templates and local read-state for message threads

This data stays on your device and is removed when you delete the app. Like other app data, it is included in your device's standard encrypted iCloud or computer backup; the app does not run any cloud sync of its own.

8. Third-Party Services

TransferWatch uses the following third-party services:

  • AeroDataBox (via API.market) — receives flight identifiers only (no personal data). Privacy policies: aerodatabox.com/privacy, api.market/privacy_policy.
  • AirLabs (optional, off by default) — receives flight identifiers only (no personal data).
  • Apple Push Notification service (only if the push relay is configured) — carries notifications from your own server to your device, per Apple's privacy policy.

Your self-hosted companion servers (Section 5) are your own infrastructure, not third parties. The app does not use analytics, advertising networks, crash reporting services, or any other third-party SDKs.

9. Children's Privacy

TransferWatch is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.

10. Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
